## GEO Content Principles

### The 5 Pillars of GEO-Ready Content

**1. Answer-First Structure**
Every section should open with a direct answer to an implied question. AI engines scan for direct, citable statements — not buried conclusions.

```
❌ Before: "Our yoga mat features premium TPE material that has been carefully 
sourced from sustainable suppliers and manufactured with precision..."

✅ After: "The NexMat Pro is a 6mm non-slip yoga mat made from eco-certified TPE. 
It's designed for hot yoga, Pilates, and daily practice."
```

**2. Specific, Citable Claims**
Vague marketing language cannot be cited. Specific facts can.

```
❌ Not citable: "Great quality, loved by thousands"
✅ Citable: "Rated 4.8/5 by over 2,300 verified customers. Keeps liquids cold for 24 hours."
```

**3. Buyer Intent Language**
Write how buyers search, not how marketers write.

```
❌ Marketing voice: "Elevate your wellness journey with our premium solution"
✅ Buyer voice: "Best for yoga beginners who want a durable, affordable mat that won't slip"
```

**4. Conversational Structure**
AI is trained on conversational data. Content that reads naturally in conversation gets cited more.

**5. Completeness**
AI avoids recommending incomplete product pages. Cover: what it is, who it's for, why it's better, what it costs, and why to trust it.

## Content Audit Before Rewriting

Assess the existing content on:

| Dimension | Check | Score |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Clarity** | Can AI identify what this product is in the first sentence? | /3 |
| **Specificity** | Are claims backed by specific data/facts? | /3 |
| **Buyer Intent** | Does content match how buyers ask questions? | /3 |
| **Completeness** | Does it cover who, what, why, how much, trust signals? | /3 |
| **Readability** | Is it conversational or stiff marketing copy? | /3 |

**Total: /15** — aim for 12+ before publishing

## Step-by-Step Optimization Workflow

### Step 1 — Content Analysis

Review the existing content and identify:
- What's working (keep and enhance)
- Keyword stuffing (rewrite naturally)
- Thin sections (expand with specific details)
- Missing buyer intent signals (add use cases)
- Vague claims (replace with specifics)
- Missing trust signals (add reviews, certifications)

### Step 2 — Structure Rewrite

Rewrite using this GEO-optimized structure:

```
[Opening Sentence] — What is this product, who is it for?
[Core Benefit] — Primary value proposition with a specific claim
[Use Cases] — 3-5 specific scenarios where this product helps
[Key Features] — Bullet list with benefit-led descriptions
[Why This One] — Differentiation from alternatives
[Trust Signals] — Reviews, certifications, warranty, social proof
[Who It's NOT For] — Honest qualification (builds trust, filters buyers)
[Call to Action] — Clear next step
```

### Step 3 — Sentence-Level GEO Optimization

**Opening sentence formula:**
```
[Product Name] is a [category] [designed/built/made] for [primary audience] 
who [primary need or problem].
```

Example:
```
"NexMat Pro is a non-slip yoga mat designed for hot yoga practitioners 
who need a stable surface that holds grip through intense sessions."
```

**Feature description formula:**
```
[Feature name]: [what it does] — [benefit to buyer]
```

Example:
```
"6mm TPE foam: Provides joint cushioning during floor poses — especially 
important for knees and wrists in longer sessions."
```

**Trust signal formula:**
```
[Specific number] [specific group] [specific action```

Example:
```
"Over 3,200 yoga instructors use NexMat Pro in their studios. 
Rated 4.9/5 for grip performance and durability."
```

## Platform-Specific GEO Content Guidelines

### Amazon Listing Content

**Title (GEO + SEO optimized):**
```
[Brand] [Product Name] — [Key Feature 1], [Key Feature 2], [Key Feature 3]
[Audience/Use Case Qualifier]
```
Example: `NexMat Pro Yoga Mat — Non-Slip, 6mm TPE Foam, Eco-Certified | For Hot Yoga & Pilates`

**Bullet Points (GEO formula):**
```
• [BENEFIT HEADLINE]: [Specific feature] that [buyer outcome]. [One supporting fact.]
```
Example:
```
• STAYS GRIP IN HOT YOGA: Moisture-activated surface increases grip as you sweat — no slipping during Bikram or flow sessions
• JOINT PROTECTION: 6mm TPE foam absorbs impact on knees and wrists — thicker than standard 4mm mats
• ECO-CERTIFIED MATERIALS: Oeko-Tex Standard 100 certified TPE — free from PVC, latex, and toxic materials
```

**Product Description (GEO optimized):**
- Open with product + audience + primary benefit
- Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)
- Include FAQ-style sections where allowed
- End with trust signal (reviews + warranty)

### Shopify Product Pages

**Description structure:**
1. Opening hook (1 sentence — product + benefit)
2. Who it's for (2-3 sentences)
3. Core features (bullet list with benefits)
4. Why choose this (comparison signals)
5. Trust section (reviews, certifications, warranty)
6. FAQ section (5-8 Q&As)

**Character limits:**
- Meta description: 150-160 characters
- Title tag: 50-60 characters

### Category Pages

Category pages are often thin — a major GEO miss. Add:
- Opening paragraph explaining the category and who it's for
- Buying guide section covering key decision factors
- FAQ content covering category-level questions
- Sub-category navigation with descriptive labels
