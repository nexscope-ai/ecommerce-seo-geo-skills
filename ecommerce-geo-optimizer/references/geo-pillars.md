# Ecommerce GEO readiness framework

Use this framework as a qualitative diagnostic aid, not as a claim about proprietary AI ranking factors. Readiness and measured AI visibility are different outcomes.

## Contents

1. Access and search foundation
2. Product facts and identity
3. Structured data and consistency
4. Buyer-intent coverage
5. Trust and proof
6. Answerability and content structure
7. Media and page experience
8. External visibility evidence
9. Optional heuristic scoring

## 1. Access and search foundation

Verify accessibility, indexability, canonicalization, internal discovery, rendered text, and observed page experience. Do not infer technical status from copy alone.

## 2. Product facts and identity

Make the product, brand, category, audience, use cases, specifications, variants, price, availability, and policies clear and mutually consistent. Keep important facts in text as well as media.

## 3. Structured data and consistency

Use accurate Product and Offer-family markup where the platform permits it. Structured data may support understanding and eligible search features, but it does not guarantee rankings, rich results, AI citations, or recommendations.

## 4. Buyer-intent coverage

Cover relevant fit, use cases, limitations, compatibility, value, alternatives, shipping, returns, warranty, and support questions using real evidence when available.

## 5. Trust and proof

Use genuine reviews, clear seller identity, accurate policies, traceable certifications or tests, and consistent third-party evidence. Never manufacture proof.

## 6. Answerability and content structure

Use direct product explanations, specific facts, descriptive headings, concise answers to genuine buyer questions, and honest limitations. FAQ formatting can improve usability but is not a universal citation mechanism.

## 7. Media and page experience

Provide relevant crawlable media, accessibility-focused alt text, responsive delivery, observed performance improvements, and text alternatives for important visual information.

## 8. External visibility evidence

Measure actual mentions, ranks, citations, and competitors with explicit queries, models, markets, languages, and run dates. Do not infer visibility from readiness.

## 9. Optional heuristic scoring

When explicitly requested and supported by evidence, use `0` for weak, `1` for partial, `2` for strong, and `Unknown` for uninspected. Report unknowns separately and never forecast citations, traffic, or sales from the score.
